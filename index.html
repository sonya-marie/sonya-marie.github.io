Sonya Marie Lustig

Published on October 17th, 2019

Sonya is a second year Jouralism and Design major and Hispanic Studies minor at Eugene Lang College. Although writing is Sonya's favorite artistic medium, she also enjoys making collages and printmaking.

Collages

To the right is a collage, OAK, I originally created with paper and later scanned and digitally altered using Photoshop. The background and circle were cut out of a 1960's Life magazine, and the "Oak" text was cut from a luggage tag flying to Oakland International Airport fifteen minutes from my childhood home in California.

Poetry

muscle
i went walking in my stone
back to the white stripe
the milk in the shallow
past of me
each time i visit
oaxaca seems more
and more martian
the jacaranda
growing lower
weaves its root into dirt
there is a light stepping over
my feet
stepping over
the cobblestone

Publications

Sonya's work has been graciously featured on a number of in print and online publications, from Nomenclatter, to school newspaper O.S.A. Telegraph, and more. Sonya has also helped co-produce the Enizagam Fiction and Poetry Anthology , as well as self-published a book of poems and personal essays entitled All Things Burn Black in 2017.
